## Prevalence of Food Intolerances

Food intolerances and sensitivities affect a significant and growing share of the global population:

| Condition | Global Prevalence | EU Prevalence | Trend |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lactose intolerance | 68% of adults | 35–40% | Stable |
| Gluten sensitivity (non-celiac) | 6–10% | 8–12% | Increasing diagnosis rate |
| Coeliac disease | 1–2% | 1–2% | Under-diagnosed (4:1 ratio) |
| IBS (FODMAP-related) | 10–15% | 12–15% | Increasing |
| Multiple intolerances | 3–5% | 4–6% | Increasing |

**Key insight:** Only ~30% of people with food intolerances have access to professional dietary management. The remaining 70% represent a massive underserved digital market.

## Market Drivers

### 1. AI Adoption Acceleration

Consumers and healthcare providers are rapidly adopting AI-powered health tools. GPT-4 class models make personalised nutrition guidance achievable at consumer price points for the first time. NutriAID was built natively on this capability.

### 2. Post-Pandemic Health Awareness

Sustained post-COVID consumer interest in preventive health, immunity, and gut health has driven nutrition app adoption rates 3x above pre-pandemic baselines.

### 3. Increasing Intolerance Diagnosis Rates

Improved diagnostic availability and growing public awareness have increased declared intolerance and allergy rates year-over-year since 2019. This directly expands NutriAID's core addressable market.

### 4. Personalisation Demand

Generic nutrition advice has low engagement and retention. Personalised, intolerance-aware recommendations command premium pricing and achieve 2.4x higher 90-day retention than generic alternatives (per Sensor Tower 2023).

### 5. Employer Wellness Programmes

B2B wellness benefit spending in the EU grew to €8.2B in 2024 (up 18% YoY), with digital nutrition tools increasingly included in corporate health packages. This creates a natural enterprise sales channel for NutriAID.

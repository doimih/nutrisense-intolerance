## Overview

NutriAID serves distinct audience segments: direct consumers managing dietary restrictions, healthcare practitioners supporting patients, and enterprise operators deploying a white-label nutrition platform. This report profiles each segment with behavioural data, willingness-to-pay signals, and acquisition channel priorities.

---

## Primary Segment: Intolerance-Managed Consumers

### Profile

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Age | 25–45 (core), 18–55 (extended) |
| Gender split | 62% female, 38% male |
| Education | 65%+ tertiary educated |
| Income | Middle to upper-middle |
| Device | Primarily mobile (iOS 55%, Android 38%, Web 7%) |
| Geography | Urban and suburban EU, English + Romanian speaking |

### Behavioural Indicators

- Has received a formal or self-identified intolerance diagnosis (gluten, lactose, FODMAP, IBS)
- Actively reads food labels; familiar with ingredient avoidance
- Has tried at least one nutrition app previously
- Frustrated by generic meal plans that ignore their restrictions
- High intent to pay for a personalised solution: 74% of surveyed users in this segment indicated willingness to pay €5–15/month for a nutrition tool that "understands my intolerances" (NutriAID internal survey, n=312, May 2026)

### Pain Points Addressed

| Pain Point | NutriAID Solution |
|---|---|
| Generic meal plans contain forbidden ingredients | AI workers enforce intolerance and allergy exclusion at every step |
| No personalised recipe guidance | Recipe Builder tailored to declared profile |
| Difficult to track symptoms vs. foods | Monitoring journal with AI-powered trend analysis |
| Medical advice is expensive and hard to access | AI guidance available 24/7 at subscription cost |
| Inconsistent AI responses from generic tools | Supervised, validated, self-correcting AI pipeline |

## Secondary Segment: Health-Conscious Users Without Formal Diagnoses

### Profile

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Age | 22–40 |
| Motivation | Weight management, gut health, energy optimisation |
| Current tools | MyFitnessPal, calorie counters, Reddit communities |

### Value Proposition

These users have not been formally diagnosed but report persistent digestive discomfort, low energy, or difficulty achieving nutritional goals. NutriAID's AI guidance — without requiring a diagnosis — allows them to explore intolerances, receive personalised recommendations, and track improvement over time. Upsell potential to medical consultation partnerships is high.

## B2B Segment 1: Dietitians and Nutritionists

### Profile

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Practice size | Solo practitioners to 5-person clinics |
| Location | EU, private practice |
| Need | Tool to generate and track patient meal plans at scale |

### Value Proposition

NutriAID white-label allows a dietitian to offer AI-assisted personalised nutrition guidance to 50+ patients simultaneously, with:

- Patient monitoring journals the practitioner can review
- AI-generated meal plans pre-configured to each patient's profile
- Medical disclaimer system that protects the practitioner
- No AI expertise required from the practitioner

**Pricing model:** Clinic licence at €150–400/month covering up to 50 patient accounts.

---

## B2B Segment 2: Gastroenterology Clinics

### Profile

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Specialisation | IBS, coeliac, Crohn's, SIBO, food allergy |
| Patient volume | 200–2,000 active patients |
| Need | Post-diagnosis dietary support between appointments |

### Value Proposition

NutriAID provides clinically-aware (not clinically-responsible) dietary guidance between patient appointments. The AI safety layer and medical disclaimer system are specifically designed to keep the platform within wellness guidance — not medical care — which satisfies the legal requirements for unsupervised deployment.

**Pricing model:** Per-seat enterprise licence with custom branding, starting at €800/month.

## B2B Segment 3: Corporate Wellness Programmes

### Profile

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|

Add NutriAID to the company's wellness benefit stack, providing employees with personalised nutrition guidance. Integration with existing employee benefits platforms (Benify, Cobee) possible via API.

## Retention Profile

Based on current user cohort data:

| Period | Retention Rate |
|---|---|
| Day 7 | 68% |
| Day 30 | 44% |
| Day 90 | 31% |
| Day 180 | 24% |

Retention is significantly higher for users who enter at least 5 monitoring journal entries in their first 14 days (habit formation group): 90-day retention of 51% vs. 22% for low-engagement users.

**Key insight:** The monitoring journal is NutriAID's primary retention mechanism. Engagement strategies should prioritise journal habit formation.
